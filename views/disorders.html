Types of Sleep Disorders

Sleep disorders encompass a range of conditions that affect the quality, timing, or duration of sleep, impacting a personâ€™s ability to function properly while awake. Understanding these disorders is crucial for managing symptoms and improving overall health.

The International Classification of Sleep Disorders (ICSD) categorized sleep disorders based on the symptoms, how it affects a person (pathophysiology) and the body system it affects. The brand-new revision to the third edition, ICSD-3R includes the following categories:

Insomnia

Description: Difficulty falling or staying asleep.

Causes: Stress, anxiety, depression, lifestyle factors, medications, caffeine, nicotine.

Symptoms: Trouble falling asleep, frequent awakenings, waking up too early, daytime fatigue.

Treatments: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I), medications, lifestyle changes, sleep hygiene.

Sleep Apnea

Description: Interrupted breathing during sleep.

Types:

  • Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA): Blockage of the airway.
  • Central Sleep Apnea (CSA): Brain fails to signal the muscles to breathe.

Symptoms: Loud snoring, gasping for air during sleep, daytime sleepiness, morning headaches.

Treatments: Continuous Positive Airway Pressure (CPAP), lifestyle changes, weight loss, surgery.

Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS)

Description: Uncomfortable sensations in the legs with an urge to move them.

Causes: Often unknown, can be linked to chronic diseases, iron deficiency, medications.

Symptoms: Discomfort in the legs, worse at night, relief with movement.

Treatments: Medications, lifestyle changes, iron supplements, leg massages.

Diagnosis and Tests for Sleep Issues

Understanding and addressing sleep disorders often involves diagnostic tests to identify the specific condition and its severity.

Sleep Study (Polysomnography)

Description: Comprehensive test to diagnose sleep disorders.

Procedure: Conducted overnight in a sleep lab; monitors brain waves, blood oxygen levels, heart rate, breathing, eye and leg movements.

Uses: Diagnoses sleep apnea, restless legs syndrome, narcolepsy, parasomnias, and other sleep disorders.

Preparation: Avoid caffeine and alcohol before the test; follow specific instructions from the sleep lab.

Home Sleep Tests

Description: Simplified sleep study conducted at home.

Procedure: Portable device monitors breathing, oxygen levels, heart rate, and snoring.

Uses: Primarily used to diagnose obstructive sleep apnea.

Preparation: Follow the device instructions; ensure a typical sleep environment.

Multiple Sleep Latency Test (MSLT)

Description: Measures daytime sleepiness and how quickly one falls asleep in a quiet environment during the day.

Procedure: Conducted the day after an overnight sleep study; involves five scheduled naps every two hours.

Uses: Diagnoses narcolepsy and idiopathic hypersomnia.

Preparation: Avoid caffeine and stimulants before the test; follow specific instructions from the sleep center.
