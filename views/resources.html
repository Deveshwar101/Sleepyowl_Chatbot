FAQs

  • What is insomnia?

    Insomnia is a sleep disorder characterized by difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or both, despite having the opportunity to sleep. It can be caused by stress, anxiety, depression, or certain medications.

  • How much sleep do I need?

    The recommended amount of sleep varies by age:
    - Adults: 7-9 hours per night
    - Teenagers: 8-10 hours per night
    - Children (6-12 years): 9-12 hours per night

  • What are the symptoms of sleep apnea?

    Symptoms of sleep apnea include loud snoring, gasping for air during sleep, daytime sleepiness, and morning headaches. It can significantly impact overall health if left untreated.

  • How can I improve my sleep hygiene?

    Improving sleep hygiene involves adopting habits that promote good sleep, such as maintaining a regular sleep schedule, creating a comfortable sleep environment, and avoiding caffeine and screens before bedtime.

  • What treatments are available for narcolepsy?

    Treatments for narcolepsy may include medications to improve alertness and scheduled naps throughout the day. Lifestyle adjustments and maintaining a consistent sleep schedule are also important.
